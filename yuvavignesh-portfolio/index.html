// About

Who I Am

I'm an R&D-focused Mechanical Engineer pursuing a Master's in Robotics & Autonomous Systems at Arizona State University. My work sits at the intersection of medical device development, robotic systems, and data-driven design validation — where I turn first-principles engineering into tangible devices that improve people's lives.

From designing wearable rehabilitation systems with Barrow Neurological Institute to building additively manufactured implants with optimized biomechanics, I bring hands-on prototyping, rigorous V&V testing, and cross-functional collaboration to every project. I'm equally comfortable running FEA simulations in ANSYS, writing Python for computer vision, and soldering circuits on a bench.

Currently, I'm a Graduate Research Associate at ASU's HAPT-X Lab, developing wearable camera systems and ML-driven action recognition for hand-object interaction research. Beyond engineering, I serve as the Officer of Photography for ISA, capturing campus life through my lens.
